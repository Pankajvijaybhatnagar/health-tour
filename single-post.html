Ayurvedic Therapies

Abhyanga (Therapeutic Massage)

Abhyanga involves the application of warm herbal oils to the body, followed by a gentle massage. This therapy nourishes the tissues, improves circulation, and promotes relaxation, leaving you feeling rejuvenated and refreshed

Swedana (Herbal Steam Therapy)

Swedana entails the use of herbal steam to induce sweating, aiding in the elimination of toxins from the body. This therapy helps alleviate muscle stiffness, joint pain, and respiratory congestion, promoting detoxification and relaxation.

Shirodhara (Oil Dripping Therapy)

It involves the continuous pouring of warm herbal oil over the forehead, stimulating the third eye chakra and inducing a profound sense of relaxation. This therapy calms the mind, relieves stress, and enhances mental clarity and focus.

Nasya (Nasal Administration Therapy)

Nasya involves the administration of herbal oils or powders into the nasal passages. This therapy helps alleviate sinus congestion, headaches, and respiratory ailments, while also promoting mental clarity and cognitive function.

Janu Basti (Knee Therapy)

It targets the knee joints, employing the same principle of containment with warm herbal oil. This therapy is effective in reducing knee pain, inflammation, and stiffness, while also promoting flexibility and mobility in the knee joints.

Kati Basti (Lower Back Therapy)

It involves the containment of warm herbal oil within a specially constructed boundary on the lower back area. This therapy is beneficial for relieving lower back pain, sciatica, and stiffness, while also nourishing the muscles and supporting spinal health.

Netra Tarpana (Eye Rejuvenation Therapy)

It involves the gentle bathing of the eyes with herbal ghee, promoting relaxation and rejuvenation of the eyes and surrounding tissues. This therapy helps alleviate eye strain, dryness, and irritation, while also improving vision and promoting overall eye health.

Pinda Sweda (Herbal Bolus Massage)

It involves the application of warm herbal boluses to the body, followed by a massage. This therapy helps alleviate muscle pain, stiffness, and inflammation, while also promoting detoxification.
